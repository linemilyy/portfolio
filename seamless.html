Seamless

Overview

For our final project in our program, we were instructed to create a solution for a currrent problem. After many brainstormed ideas, my team and I decided on creating a solution to the gap between shopper and store owners during the Covid-19 pandemic. Our final decision was to create Seamless, a Shopify integration that easily books in-store try on appointments for shoppers via online clothing websites.

We had 13 days to complete this project and we worked together in and out of class to bring our idea to reality.

    User Reserach

    What are current shopping habits of customers?

    During our interviews and surveys, we discovered that people have switched to online shopping during this Covid-19 pandemic despite their preference of shopping in person before the pandemic.

    From our intervies, we gathered a variety of insights from users and created an affinity map to narrow down the feedback.

    User Insight

    From our interviews and surveys, we discovered two main painpoints from our users about online shopping that regarded the uncertainty of sizing and inability to see products in person.

    Competitor Analysis

    To help up build our app, we did some competitor research to see if there were any existing apps similar to our ideas, and if so, if there were any problems with existing apps. After finding a lot of shopping and scheduling apps, we conducted a competitor analysis to see how to proceed with our vision.

    Problem Statement

    How might we enable apparel boutique owners to seamlessly provide a combination of safe in-store and meaningful online interactions with customers so that they can continue generating revenue measured by an increase in sales, reduction in return rate and increased customer satisfaction?

    Ideation

    To start off our ideation process, we got together and each created moodboards to invision the path we would like to take for our app. The instpiration we got from our boards soon drove us into createing a storyboard.

    As our storyboard was set, we began to empthasize with our user and created a journey map to see where potential pain points could exist. Knowing the pain points, we than began to defining the user flow and sketching out wireframes.

    UI Branding

    As a team, we wanted to go with a sophisticated yet classy feel for our app while making it versatile so that it does not take away from the clothing websites that the inegration may be part of.

    Iteration

    User Test Insights

    From our user tests, we noticed where information to the user was getting lost and we made sure to fix the following callouts:

  • consistent branding throughout the entire process
  • add drop down menus on the sizing page for ease of use
  • view and manage your appointments on the backend for store owners
  • move CTA closer to the botttom of the screen for better user access
  • make adjustments to colors so that they pass color contrast tests
  • move labels above corresponding input fields to avoid potential confusion

    Reflections

    With more time, my team and I decided that we would like to further expand Seamless with the following:

  • Offer several branding options that stores can choose from (this would allow for customizable buttons, colors, and fonts to match the store owner’s website)
  • Allow shoppers to view their account info, appointment history, sizing info, etc
  • Add more options for sizing (men’s, petite/tall, plus, shoe widths, etc)
  • Build out the body scanning user flow and the virtual appointment flow
