Seamless

Overview

For the final project in our program, my team and I decided to create a solution for the current gap between shoppers and store owners during the Covid-19 pandemic. Our final decision was to create Seamless, a Shopify integration that easily books in-store try on appointments for shoppers via online clothing websites.

We had 13 days to complete this project, and we worked together in and out of class to bring our idea to reality.

    User Reserach

    To begin our research process, my team and I drafted our objective:

    Safely connect shoppers and small apparel boutique owners to optimize the buying and selling of products while still maintaining customer satisfaction and sales through personalized virtual appointments.

    From our intervies, we gathered a variety of insights from users and created an affinity map to narrow down the feedback.

    Once we finalized on our objective, we started to build out interview questions that that targeted both shoppers and shop owners, with our main point of answering the following question:

    What are the current shopping habits of customers?

    From our interviews, we gathered a variety of insights from users and created an affinity diagram to categorize our feedback.

    Affinity Diagram

    User Insight

    During our interviews and surveys, we discovered that people have switched to online shopping during this Covid-19 pandemic, despite their preference of in-store shopping.

    Survey Results

    Competitor Analysis

    To help start the process of building out Seamless, we looked at potential competitors. After finding a handful of shopping and scheduling apps, we conducted a competitor analysis to see the strengths and weaknesses encapsulated in each competitor.

    Problem Statement

    How might we enable apparel boutique owners to seamlessly provide a combination of safe in-store and meaningful online interactions with customers so that they can continue generating revenue measured by an increase in sales, reduction in return rate and increased customer satisfaction?

    Ideation

    Kicking off our ideation process, we got together and created individual moodboards for our vision. As we had many similar qualities across our boards, we easily decided on a theme which then drove us straight into creating a storyboard.

    Storyboard

    As our storyboard was set, we began to empathize with our potential users and created a journey map to see where probable pain points could exist. With these pain points in mind, we then began defining a solid user flow and building out wireframes.

    Wireframes

    UI Branding

    Drawing from the similarities in our moodboards, we fine tuned our colors and chose fonts that portrayed a sophisticated yet classy feeling to our app. We went with more neutral toned colors to make sure Seamless would be able to easily integrate with multiple clothing webpages without taking away from the clothing itself.

    Iteration

    From our user tests on our Lofi prototype, we got a clear understanding of where users were getting lost. To fix these pockets of uncertainty, we made sure address the following callouts in our future iterations:

  • - Inconsistent branding throughout the pages
  • - Add drop down menus on the sizing page for ease of use
  • - View and manage your appointments on the backend for store owners
  • - Move CTA buttons closer to the bottom of the screen for better access
  • - Make adjustments to colors so that they pass color contrast tests
  • - Move labels above corresponding input fields to avoid confusion

    Reflections

    With more time, my team and I would expand Seamless and further iterate on the following:

  • - Offer several branding options so that stores could customize Seamless to match their website theme
  • - Allow shoppers to view their account info, appointment history, sizing information, ect.
  • - Add more options for sizing (mens, petite, tall, plus, shoe widths)
  • - Build out the body scanning and virtual appointment user flows
